French Toast

Description

French toast is a classic breakfast dish loved by many. It's simple yet satisfying. To make it, you typically soak slices of bread in a mixture of beaten eggs, milk and often a bit of sugar and vanilla for flavor.

Its origin can be traced back to ancient times. The dish like originated as a way to utilize stale bread preventing waste. In fact, references to a dish resembling French toast can be found in ancient Roman cookbooks.

The earliest version of French toast were simple.: stale bread dipped in a mixture of milk and eggs, then fried.

Ingredients

  • 2/3 cup milk
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract (Optional)
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon (Optional)
  • salt to taste
  • 6 thick slices bread
  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter, or more as needed

Steps

  1. Gather all ingredients
  2. Whisk mile, eggs, vanilla, cinnamon and salt together in a shallow bowl.
  3. Lightly butter a griddle or skillet and heat over medium-high heat.
  4. Dunk bread in the egg mixture, soaking both sides.
  5. Transfer to the hot skillet and cook until golden, 3 to 4 minutes per side.
  6. Serve hot.
